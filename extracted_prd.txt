AI Agent Requirements Document - Pega Account Research Purpose: This document defines the step-by-step process an AI agent must follow to research a company account for sales purposes. The agent must follow these steps in exact order, be accurate at every stage, and populate a structured Excel output at the end using the column headings defined in Section 13. STEP 1 — Input &amp; Classification (Pega Partner vs. Customer) Input: The agent receives a company name. The agent cross-references this company name against the provided Excel file which contains a list of Pega partners and Pega customers. If the company is classified as a Pega Partner → STOP. Do not proceed. Record "Partner" in Column 9 (Pega Customer / Partner) and leave all remaining columns blank. If the company is classified as a Pega Customer → Record "Customer" in Column 9 and continue to Step 2. Accuracy note : This classification must be exact. The agent must not guess or assume — it must match the company name against the Excel data carefully, accounting for possible name variations (e.g., abbreviations, "Inc.", "Ltd." suffixes). STEP 2 — Website Research &amp; Revenue Classification The agent visits the company's official website and reviews the products and services they offer. Goal: Determine whether the company's primary source of revenue comes from selling software or from something else. If the website is unclear or insufficient, the agent must query an LLM with the prompt: "What does [Company Name] do and what is its primary source of revenue?" Classification: Software — The company's core business is building and selling software products. Example: Autodesk. Non-Software — The company's core business is something else entirely and software is just a supporting tool. Example: Tokio Marine HCC (primary revenue is from insurance). Populate: Column 6 — Primary Revenue Source (brief description, e.g. "Insurance premiums", "Enterprise software licensing") Column 7 — Software or Non-Software (write exactly: "Software" or "Non-Software") STEP 3 — Basic Firmographics From the company website and/or LLM or web search, collect the following: Industry → Column 4 Headquarters location (city and country) → Column 5 Annual revenue in USD → Column 8 STEP 4 — Corporate Structure (Parent Company &amp; Subsidiaries) The agent searches Google with: "[Company Name] subsidiaries and parent company" Confirm results from multiple sources where possible. Populate: Column 2 — Parent Company (name of parent, or write "None — is the parent company" if applicable) Column 3 — India Subsidiary (name of the India-based subsidiary if one exists, otherwise "None") Column 12 — Subsidiaries &amp; Associated Companies (list all subsidiaries with their country/region) Rules: If the input company is itself the parent/main company → note this in Column 2 and continue normally. If an India-based subsidiary exists → this becomes the primary focus entity for all subsequent steps. All India-specific data will be collected from this entity. If the India subsidiary has no LinkedIn page → fall back to the parent company's LinkedIn page for those steps. STEP 5 — GCC (Global Capability Centre) Check in India The agent performs the following Google searches: "[Company Name] GCC in India" "[Company Name] operations in India" "[Company Name] capability centres in India" If GCCs exist, populate: Column 13 — GCCs in India → "Yes" Column 14 — Number of GCCs → (total count) Column 15 — GCC Locations → (list all cities) Column 16 — Main GCC in India → (the GCC with the highest employee count) If no GCCs exist: Column 13 → "No" Columns 14, 15, 16 → "N/A" Skip to Step 6. STEP 6 — LinkedIn Page Discovery &amp; Verification The agent searches Google with: "[Company Name] LinkedIn" This must be done for all relevant entities — the input company, the India subsidiary (prioritised), and the parent company. On each LinkedIn page, the agent confirms: Industry → cross-check against Column 4. Flag any mismatch in Column 33 (Research Notes). Headquarters → cross-check against Column 5. Flag any mismatch in Column 33. If the India subsidiary has no LinkedIn page, use the parent company's LinkedIn page for all India-related steps going forward. Note this in Column 33. STEP 7 — Total Employee Count On the LinkedIn pages of all relevant entities, record the total displayed employee count. Populate: Column 22 — Total Employee Count (Org-wide) Column 23 — Employee Count (India) (apply India location filter on LinkedIn People section) STEP 8 — Engineering, IT, QA &amp; SDET Headcount This step is performed on the LinkedIn People section of all relevant entities. Process: Go to the People section of the company's LinkedIn page. On the right side, check if Engineering or IT counts are listed directly — if yes, use those numbers. If not, type "Engineering" in the search bar, then under "What they do" select Engineering, then remove the search keyword to get the actual count. Repeat for "IT" , "QA" , and "SDET" . For India-specific counts, apply a location filter for India and repeat all of the above. Populate: Column 24 — Engineering Count (Org-wide) Column 25 — Engineering Count (India) Column 26 — IT Count (Org-wide) Column 27 — IT Count (India) Column 28 — SDET &amp; QA Count (Org-wide) Column 29 — SDET &amp; QA Count (India) Column 30 — Engineering % of Total Headcount (formula: Engineering Count Org-wide ÷ Total Employee Count Org-wide × 100, rounded to 1 decimal place) STEP 9 — Pega Usage Verification This is a multi-layered check performed across all relevant entities. 9a. LinkedIn Keyword Search In the LinkedIn People section, search for each of the following keywords one by one: "Pega" "CSSA" "LSA" "PRPC" "Pega QA" "Lead System Architect" "Digital Process Automation" For each result, open several individual profiles and verify: Is Pega part of their current role at this company? Or is it only mentioned in past experience? Confirmation threshold: A minimum of 5–10 people must have Pega clearly in their current designation or current role (e.g., Pega Developer, Pega QA Engineer, Pega LSA) to mark usage as confirmed. 9b. Google Confirmation (if LinkedIn is inconclusive) If LinkedIn does not surface enough evidence, search Google: "[Company Name] Pega" Check the date and credibility of any source found. Only treat as confirmation if the source is recent and reliable. Populate: Column 10 — Pega Usage Confirmed → "Yes" / "No" / "Unconfirmed" Column 11 — Pega Evidence → (e.g., "12 LinkedIn profiles with current Pega roles" or "Google result: [source name], dated [date]") STEP 10 — Competing Platform Check On the LinkedIn People pages of all relevant entities, repeat the same keyword search process from Step 9 for: "ServiceNow" "Salesforce" "SAP" Record which platforms have a confirmed presence. Populate: Column 31 — Other Enterprise Platforms (e.g., "ServiceNow — Yes, Salesforce — Yes, SAP — No") STEP 11 — Service Company &amp; Outsourcing Check Google searches: "[Company Name] Accenture" "[Company Name] TCS" "[Company Name] Infosys" "[Company Name] Wipro" "[Company Name] service companies" Continue with other major IT service firms as needed. LinkedIn check: On the People section of the company and parent company, search for employees affiliated with Accenture, TCS, Infosys, Wipro, etc. Jobs check: Review the LinkedIn Jobs section. Note whether the company is actively hiring for Software Developer, QA Engineer, Test Engineer, or similar technical roles. Record the exact designations found. Populate: Column 17 — Software Development Model → "In-house" / "Outsourced" / "Mixed" Column 18 — Signals of Service Companies → "Yes" / "No" Column 19 — Service Companies Identified → (list names found, e.g., "TCS, Infosys") Column 20 — Hiring for Tech Roles → "Yes" / "No" Column 21 — Tech Roles Being Hired For → (list designations, e.g., "Pega Developer, QA Engineer, SDET") STEP 12 — Final Categorization Using all data collected across Steps 1–11, the agent assigns one of the following categories. E1 — Fully Outsourced The company has completely outsourced all software development and testing to service companies. There is little to no internal engineering team and no significant hiring activity for technical roles. Signals: High service company presence, low engineering headcount, no or minimal tech hiring. E1.1 — Transitioning In-House The company currently outsources but is actively working to bring software development back in-house. There is a growing internal engineering team AND active hiring for technical roles on LinkedIn. Signals: Service company presence confirmed, but engineering headcount is meaningful AND tech roles are being hired for. E2 — Non-Software Enterprise The company's primary business is not software. Revenue comes from another industry such as insurance, banking, manufacturing, or retail. They may have a digital or technology function but it exists in support of the core business, not as the core business itself. Examples: Tokio Marine HCC (insurance), Newfold Digital (domain/hosting). E3 — Software-First Enterprise The company's primary business is software. They have a strong internal engineering culture and generally prefer to build over buy. However, they remain approachable for enterprise platforms like Pega, Salesforce, Mendix , or OutSystems . Examples: Google, Amazon. Populate: Column 32 — Enterprise Type → "E1" / "E1.1" / "E2" / "E3" STEP 13 — Populate Research Notes For any step where the agent encountered conflicting data, low-confidence findings, missing information, or had to make an assumption, this must be clearly noted.
